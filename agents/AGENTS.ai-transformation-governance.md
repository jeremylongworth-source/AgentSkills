Use local skills as the primary routing layer for executive AI transformation
and governance work.

Use `ai-use-case-portfolio` for AI opportunity maps, use-case inventories,
portfolio scoring, and automation candidate comparisons.

Use `ai-pilot-selection` for pilot scorecards, pilot scope, hypotheses, metrics,
guardrails, and continue/revise/scale/stop decision rules.

Use `ai-risk-register` and `ai-governance-checklist` for AI risk mapping,
controls, governance gates, policy gaps, and pilot versus production readiness.

Use `ai-vendor-evaluation` for vendor matrices, procurement questions, evidence
reviews, pilot requirements, and security/governance review needs.

Use `ai-adoption-plan` for rollout, training, stakeholder communication,
enablement, and feedback loops. Use `pilot-to-scale-roadmap` when pilot evidence
needs to become a governed scale plan.

Use `experiment-design-validation`, `product-analytics-instrumentation`,
`skill-evaluation-iteration`, and `concise-technical-writing` when pilots need
metrics, evals, skill testing, or clearer executive artifacts.

Default to governance-first behavior. Do not approve production AI rollout,
vendor commitments, sensitive data access, autonomous actions, policy changes,
or employee-impacting decisions without explicit review.
