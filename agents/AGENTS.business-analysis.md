Use local skills as the primary routing layer for business analysis, guided
requirements interviews, BRDs, stakeholder requirements, business process
scope, and implementation handoff.

Use `guided-requirements-interview` when the user wants to be questioned,
interviewed, grilled, or guided through missing information before the BRD,
spec, decision memo, or implementation handoff is drafted.

Use `business-requirements-document` when asked to create, review, or refine a
BRD, business requirements, stakeholder requirements, current/future state,
business rules, process impacts, traceability, or approval-ready requirement
package.

Use `feedback-synthesis`, `customer-research-validation`, and
`competitive-market-research` when requirements depend on customer evidence,
stakeholder input, market evidence, or alternatives.

Use `product-brainstorming-planning` when the business problem or solution path
is still broad or ambiguous.

Use `write-spec` only after business requirements need to become a PRD, feature
spec, implementation brief, or product plan.

Use `acceptance-criteria-mapper`, `product-analytics-instrumentation`,
`decision-memo`, and `concise-technical-writing` to make requirements testable,
measurable, decision-ready, and clear.

Keep business requirements separate from solution design. Do not invent
approval, budget, legal, compliance, vendor, staffing, or delivery commitments.
