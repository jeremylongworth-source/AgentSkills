Use local skills as the primary routing layer for creator AI production
workflows.

Use `ai-media-prompt-brief` for provider-neutral image, video, audio,
voiceover, thumbnail, b-roll, and batch media prompt briefs with creative
intent, source references, constraints, exclusions, rights/provenance notes,
acceptance criteria, and review gates.

Use `ai-video-prompt-pack` when a video generation request needs
platform/model constraints, scene timing, prompt variants, negative prompts,
safe areas, regeneration notes, or provider-specific checks for tools such as
Gemini/Veo, Sora, Runway, Pika, or Luma.

Use `social-asset-production` for profile images, banners, post images,
article covers, thumbnails, short-video covers, crop-safe prompts, export
checks, and accessibility notes for social creative.

Use `synthetic-character-bible` for AI-assisted personas, virtual influencers,
fictional hosts, mascots, synthetic spokescharacters, identity boundaries,
voice/visual continuity, prohibited claims, disclosure rules, likeness/voice
risk, and audience-trust guardrails.

Use `creator-reputation-risk-review`, `publishing-checklist`,
`usage-rights-checklist`, `ai-risk-register`, and `ai-governance-checklist` for
rights, copyright, AI labeling, platform-policy, brand-safety, likeness, voice,
consent, provenance, and human-review checks.

Use `video-script`, `short-form-hooks`, `thumbnail-title-brief`,
`caption-writer`, `podcast-outline`, `content-calendar`,
`creator-production-ops-brief`, and `concise-technical-writing` for scripts,
hooks, thumbnails, captions, voiceover/podcast structures, calendars, handoffs,
and polished production briefs.

Keep generation providers, social platforms, publishing tools, storage,
sponsor systems, and production tools read-only unless the user approves a
specific action. Do not claim legal clearance, copyright clearance, platform
compliance, disclosure sufficiency, likeness consent, voice rights, or sponsor
approval.
