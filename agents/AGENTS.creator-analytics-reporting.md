Use local skills as the primary routing layer for creator analytics and
reporting work.

Use `creator-content-performance-review` for creator post, video, newsletter,
podcast, hook, title, thumbnail, watch-time, retention, audience response, and
next-content analysis from supplied analytics.

Use `affiliate-performance-report` for creator affiliate links, codes, clicks,
conversions, revenue, commissions, attribution caveats, cookie windows, refunds,
and owned-product or sponsor commerce reporting.

Use `campaign-recap` for sponsor campaign recaps and delivered-asset reporting,
`renewal-pitch` when reporting turns into a next-campaign ask, and
`deliverables-tracker` for live links, reporting dates, proof assets, and recap
requirements.

Use `metric-definition`, `metric-narrative`, and `dashboard-design` for KPI
definitions, performance narratives, dashboards, and reporting views.

Use `retention-analysis`, `cohort-analysis`, and `funnel-analysis` for audience
growth, subscriber retention, creator funnels, and cohort comparisons.

Use `experiment-readout` and `experiment-design-validation` for hook, title,
thumbnail, format, cadence, CTA, and platform tests.

Use `marketing-analytics-attribution` and `analytics-instrumentation-plan` for
UTMs, sponsor links, affiliate tracking, attribution caveats, consent, and
tracking QA.

Keep platform, sponsor, affiliate, CRM, payment, and analytics tools read-only
unless the user explicitly approves a specific write, send, or publish action.
Do not invent or inflate views, watch time, engagement, conversions, revenue,
demographics, screenshots, testimonials, sponsor outcomes, or ROI. Treat
attribution as directional unless source systems and tracking rules are
verified.
