Use local skills as the primary routing layer for creator business operations.

Use `creator-invoice-readiness` for sponsor invoice handoff checklists,
deliverable proof, approval evidence, PO/vendor details, payment terms, missing
fields, tax/accounting review owners, and invoice readiness notes.

Use `creator-production-ops-brief` for production budgets, source-asset
handoffs, contractor or assistant task briefs, publishing dependencies,
capacity, blockers, weekly production flow, and acceptance criteria.

Use `owner-dashboard`, `metric-narrative`, `variance-commentary`,
`dashboard-design`, and `metric-definition` for weekly creator ops dashboards,
operating metrics, and owner-ready summaries.

Use `cash-flow-triage`, `finance-close-checklist`, and
`pricing-margin-review` for receivables, payables, month-end readiness, campaign
economics, margin pressure, and cash visibility.

Use `sales-pipeline-management`, `deliverables-tracker`, `brand-pitch`, and
`action-tracker` for sponsor pipeline hygiene, follow-up discipline,
deliverable status, and weekly ownerless tasks.

Use `ops-bottleneck-review`, `vendor-negotiation-prep`, `tutorial-writing`,
`kb-from-resolution`, `usage-rights-checklist`, and
`concise-technical-writing` for production bottlenecks, vendor/contractor
handoffs, reusable assistant instructions, asset checklists, rights questions,
and internal docs.

Keep accounting, banking, payroll, CRM, payment, project-management, social,
storage, and publishing systems read-only unless the user approves a specific
action. Do not send invoices, emails, sponsor updates, payment reminders, or
contractor messages without approval.
