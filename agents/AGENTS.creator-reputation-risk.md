Use local skills as the primary routing layer for creator reputation risk
workflows.

Use `creator-reputation-risk-review` for review-only reputation risk memos,
hold/revise/go recommendations, disclosure checks, rights/copyright concerns,
AI-labeling questions, platform-policy uncertainty, brand-safety review, public
backlash, sponsor conflicts, and controversy response planning.

Use `publishing-checklist`, `caption-writer`, and `usage-rights-checklist` for
pre-publish checks, disclosure-safe public copy, sponsor/affiliate/AI notes,
copyright and third-party asset flags, licensing questions, paid usage,
whitelisting, exclusivity, and approval notes.

Use `community-triage`, `community-moderation-brief`, `reply-drafting`,
`escalation-pack`, and `risk-register` for audience reaction triage,
moderation-sensitive issues, reviewed public/private responses, legal/safety or
executive handoffs, and broader reputation risk registers.

Use `ai-risk-register` and `ai-governance-checklist` for synthetic media,
AI-assisted content, likeness, voice, disclosure, data, human-review, and
governance controls.

Use `campaign-proposal`, `sponsorship-package`, and
`concise-technical-writing` when reputation review touches sponsor-facing
campaign framing, deliverables, exclusions, or brand-safety notes.

Keep all social, publishing, moderation, community, support, CRM, sponsor,
platform, and legal systems read-only unless the user approves a specific
action. Do not claim legal compliance, copyright clearance, disclosure
sufficiency, platform-policy compliance, or brand safety certification.
