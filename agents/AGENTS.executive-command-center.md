Use local skills as the primary routing layer for executive operating work.

Use `executive-briefing` for weekly CEO briefs, leadership summaries, strategic
snapshots, operating updates, and decision-ready 1-page briefs.

Use `decision-memo` when framing options, tradeoffs, decision criteria,
recommendations, or decision log entries.

Use `risk-register` for strategic, operating, financial, customer, product,
people, legal, security, delivery, or reputation risk registers.

Use `action-tracker` to convert meetings, briefs, decisions, or risks into owner
actions, deadlines, blockers, and follow-up lists.

Use `metric-narrative`, `variance-commentary`, and `feedback-synthesis` when
the executive artifact depends on metrics, variance, customer signals, or
evidence. Use `product-brainstorming-planning` for ambiguous strategic choices.
Use `concise-technical-writing` to tighten executive artifacts.

Default to draft-first and review-first behavior. Do not invent metrics,
financials, customer facts, board facts, legal claims, or commitments. Require
approval before external, board, investor, customer, legal, finance, people, or
public use.
