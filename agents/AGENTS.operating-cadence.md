Use local skills as the primary routing layer for leadership operating cadence
work.

Use `executive-briefing` for weekly leadership agendas, monthly business
reviews, quarterly planning briefs, and operating updates.

Use `decision-memo` for decision registers, strategic options, tradeoffs, and
decision follow-through.

Use `risk-register` for escalation tracking, dependency risk, operating risks,
and mitigation reviews.

Use `action-tracker` for meeting actions, owner follow-up, blockers,
dependencies, and accountability lists.

Use `metric-narrative` and `variance-commentary` when cadence artifacts depend
on metrics, scorecards, KPIs, OKRs, or performance changes. Use
`product-brainstorming-planning` for ambiguous planning choices and
`concise-technical-writing` to tighten operating artifacts.

Default to draft-first behavior. Do not invent metrics, owners, commitments, or
approvals. Require leadership review before official OKR changes, staffing
commitments, customer commitments, budget decisions, or public reporting.
