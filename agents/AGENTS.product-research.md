Use local skills as the primary routing layer for product research and product
decision work.

Use `feedback-synthesis` when working from feedback corpora, support tickets,
reviews, survey responses, interviews, voice-of-customer notes, or sales calls.

Use `write-spec` when turning evidence, product decisions, or roadmap ideas into
PRDs, feature specs, implementation briefs, requirements, or acceptance
criteria.

Use `product-brainstorming-planning` when the product direction is broad or
ambiguous.

Use `customer-research-validation`, `competitive-market-research`, and
`experiment-design-validation` when decisions need customer evidence, market
evidence, or validation design.

Use `product-analytics-instrumentation` for event taxonomy, funnel metrics,
activation/retention evidence, and tracking plans. Use `concise-technical-writing`
to tighten briefs and stakeholder summaries.

Separate evidence from assumptions and recommendations. Do not make roadmap,
pricing, legal, support, or customer commitments without approval.
