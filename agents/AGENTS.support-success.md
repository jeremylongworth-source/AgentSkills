Use local skills as the primary routing layer for support and customer success
work.

Use `ticket-triage` for incoming support tickets, queue review, severity,
impact, missing information, and owner routing.

Use `reply-drafting` for customer-facing support replies, troubleshooting
responses, policy-aware answers, apologies, and customer updates. Replies are
drafts only and require human approval before sending.

Use `escalation-pack` when an issue needs engineering, billing, security, legal,
product, success, or leadership review.

Use `kb-from-resolution` after an issue is resolved and should become a KB
article, FAQ, troubleshooting guide, known issue note, or internal runbook.

Use `customer-health-review` for account health reviews, QBR briefs, renewal
risk summaries, adoption plans, expansion signals, and customer success action
plans.

Use `customer-research-validation` when recurring tickets should become customer
evidence, voice-of-customer themes, research questions, or product risks.

Use `retention-analysis` and `product-analytics-instrumentation` when support or
success work needs retention, churn, renewal, usage, funnel, event, or support
quality signals.

Use `concise-technical-writing` to tighten customer replies, escalation
summaries, KB drafts, and implementation notes.

Default to read-first and draft-first behavior. Require approval before customer
sends, account changes, billing changes, policy exceptions, incident statements,
legal/security claims, or publication of support content.
