# Business Analysis Bundle Brief

## Problem

Teams need agents to turn stakeholder notes, process problems, and discovery
material into clear business requirements before product, vendor, engineering,
or operations teams jump into solution design.

## Target User

Business analysts, product managers, operations leads, founders, consultants,
delivery managers, and cross-functional teams preparing BRDs, requirement
packages, process-change briefs, or implementation handoffs.

## Included Skills

- `guided-requirements-interview`: run "grill me" style guided discovery that
  asks focused questions and builds aligned requirements from partial
  information.
- `business-requirements-document`: create and review BRDs, stakeholder
  requirements, business rules, scope, traceability, and approval-ready
  requirement packages.
- `feedback-synthesis`: cluster stakeholder notes, interviews, tickets, and
  feedback into evidence themes.
- `customer-research-validation`: plan and synthesize customer or stakeholder
  discovery where requirements need stronger evidence.
- `competitive-market-research`: compare alternatives, vendors, and market
  expectations when requirements depend on external context.
- `product-brainstorming-planning`: shape ambiguous business problems before
  requirements are finalized.
- `write-spec`: convert approved business requirements into PRDs, feature
  specs, or implementation briefs.
- `acceptance-criteria-mapper`: make requirements testable and reviewable.
- `product-analytics-instrumentation`: define measurement, reporting, and
  telemetry needs.
- `decision-memo`: prepare decision-ready summaries when leadership approval is
  needed.
- `concise-technical-writing`: tighten BRDs, summaries, and stakeholder
  language.

## Context Files

- `STAKEHOLDER_NOTES.md`: interview notes, workshop notes, emails, and source
  material.
- `DISCOVERY_QUESTIONS.md`: question backlog, answered questions, unresolved
  gaps, and assumptions from guided interviews.
- `CURRENT_STATE.md`: existing workflow, pain points, systems, and handoffs.
- `BUSINESS_RULES.md`: policy, process, compliance, pricing, approval, and
  operational rules.
- `DECISION_OWNERS.md`: sponsors, reviewers, approvers, and escalation path.
- `METRICS_CONTEXT.md`: current metrics, target outcomes, reporting needs, and
  known data limitations.

## MCP Preset Intent

Use documentation research for public vendor docs, regulatory/public policy
references, market context, and current tool behavior. Keep business systems,
CRM, analytics, ticketing, and document repositories read-only unless a human
reviewer explicitly approves writes.

## Safety Rules

- Keep business requirements separate from solution design.
- Do not invent stakeholder approval, legal obligations, budget authority,
  compliance status, vendor promises, or implementation commitments.
- Separate evidence, assumptions, recommendations, and open questions.
- Escalate requirements that affect billing, contracts, privacy, security,
  compliance, accessibility, staffing, customer commitments, or operational
  risk.

## Pilot Metrics

- Operational: time from stakeholder notes to first BRD draft.
- Quality: reviewer correction rate for missing scope, unclear requirements,
  and unowned decisions.
- Adoption: stakeholder approval rate after first review cycle.
- Economic: reduction in rework caused by unclear requirements.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The bundle installs with a dry run.
- BRD and guided-interview scenarios route to interview, BRD, synthesis,
  research, planning, acceptance, analytics, and writing skills as appropriate.
- Outputs separate business requirements from solution ideas and implementation
  plans.
- BRDs include scope, current/future state, business rules, traceability,
  success validation, risks, dependencies, open questions, and approval needs.
