# Executive Command Center Bundle Brief

## Problem

Executives, founders, owners, and chiefs of staff need agents to turn messy
business context into clear briefs, decisions, risks, and follow-up actions
without creating generic advice or unapproved commitments.

## Target User

CEOs, founders, small business owners, chiefs of staff, operators, and
leadership teams.

## Included Skills

- `executive-briefing`: produce weekly executive briefs and leadership
  snapshots.
- `decision-memo`: frame ambiguous decisions, options, tradeoffs, and decision
  log entries.
- `risk-register`: identify and prioritize executive risks, mitigations, owners,
  and escalation paths.
- `action-tracker`: turn briefs, meetings, decisions, and risks into owner
  follow-up lists.
- `product-brainstorming-planning`: clarify ambiguous strategic choices.
- `metric-narrative` and `variance-commentary`: explain metrics and variance
  when executive artifacts depend on numbers.
- `feedback-synthesis`: summarize customer, market, support, or team signals.
- `concise-technical-writing`: tighten executive artifacts.

## Context Files

- `EXECUTIVE_PRIORITIES.md`: current priorities, strategy, constraints, and
  non-goals.
- `OPERATING_METRICS.md`: metric definitions, sources, owners, and review
  cadence.
- `LEADERSHIP_CADENCE.md`: meeting rhythm, agenda shape, owner expectations, and
  follow-up rules.
- `EXECUTIVE_DECISION_LOG.md`: decisions, owners, rationale, review dates, and
  follow-up.
- `EXECUTIVE_RISK_TAXONOMY.md`: risk categories, likelihood/impact scales, and
  escalation rules.

## MCP Preset Intent

Use documentation research for current public facts and external context. Keep
business systems, finance systems, customer systems, board materials, and
internal operating docs read-only unless a reviewer approves a write action.

## Safety Rules

- Draft-first and review-first by default.
- Do not invent metrics, financials, customer facts, board facts, legal claims,
  staffing commitments, or strategic commitments.
- Separate facts, assumptions, recommendations, and approval needs.
- Require human approval before external, board, investor, customer, legal,
  finance, people, or public use.

## Pilot Metrics

- Operational: time to create weekly executive brief.
- Quality: reviewer correction rate and missing-context count.
- Adoption: percentage of briefs or memos used in leadership cadence.
- Economic: leadership meeting prep time saved or decision-cycle reduction.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The bundle installs with a dry run.
- Weekly brief, decision memo, risk register, and action tracker scenarios route
  to the intended skills.
- Outputs are concrete executive artifacts, not generic advice.
- Facts, assumptions, recommendations, risks, and owner actions are separated.
- External or high-stakes use requires human review.
