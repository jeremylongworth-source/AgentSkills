# Founder Fundraising IR Bundle Brief

## Problem

Founders need agents to prepare investor updates, fundraising narratives, pitch
deck outlines, data room checklists, and diligence response drafts without
inventing traction, financials, legal status, or investment claims.

## Target User

Startup founders, CEOs, investor-facing operators, chiefs of staff, and small
teams preparing fundraising or investor relations materials.

## Included Skills

- `investor-update`: draft investor updates, founder updates, traction notes,
  and investor asks.
- `fundraising-narrative`: shape fundraising stories, raise theses, and investor
  positioning.
- `pitch-deck-structure`: create slide-by-slide deck outlines and deck gap
  reviews.
- `data-room-checklist`: prepare data room indexes, readiness plans, and
  missing-item reviews.
- `diligence-response-prep`: draft diligence answers and route questions to
  reviewers.
- `executive-briefing`: summarize company context for investor-facing use.
- `metric-narrative` and `variance-commentary`: explain supplied metrics and
  changes.
- `growth-strategy-orchestration` and `marketing-positioning-strategy`: support
  GTM and positioning context.
- `concise-technical-writing`: tighten investor-facing materials.

## Context Files

- `FUNDRAISING_CONTEXT.md`: stage, raise goal, investor audience, timeline, and
  constraints.
- `INVESTOR_MESSAGING.md`: approved positioning, proof, claims, and sensitive
  language.
- `FUNDRAISING_METRICS.md`: approved metrics, definitions, sources, and review
  owners.
- `DATA_ROOM_INDEX.md`: required documents, status, sensitivity, and owners.
- `DILIGENCE_RULES.md`: reviewer routing, escalation rules, and claims requiring
  counsel or finance review.

## MCP Preset Intent

Use documentation research for current public facts and market context. Keep
CRM, finance, cap table, legal, data room, and investor communication systems
read-only unless a reviewer approves a write action.

## Safety Rules

- Analysis and drafting support only; not legal, tax, investment, accounting, or
  financial advice.
- Do not invent traction, revenue, runway, customers, contracts, investors,
  valuation, terms, legal status, or use-of-funds detail.
- Keep investor-facing drafts separate from internal notes and sensitive
  details.
- Require founder, finance, and counsel review before investor sends, diligence
  responses, data room publication, financial claims, fundraising claims, or
  term-sheet decisions.

## Pilot Metrics

- Operational: time to draft investor update or data room checklist.
- Quality: reviewer correction rate and missing-evidence count.
- Adoption: percentage of drafts used after review.
- Economic: founder prep time saved during fundraising.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The bundle installs with a dry run.
- Investor update, fundraising narrative, data room, and diligence scenarios
  route to the intended skills.
- Outputs separate sendable text, internal notes, missing evidence, and required
  reviewers.
- Financial, legal, investment, tax, and diligence-sensitive claims require
  human review.
