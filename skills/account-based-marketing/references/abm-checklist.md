# ABM Checklist

## Account Selection

- Target account list is agreed by sales and marketing.
- Selection criteria include fit, value, intent, relationship, strategic value, and ability to win.
- Accounts are tiered by value and personalization capacity.
- Account exclusions are explicit so ABM effort is not diluted.
- Existing customer, expansion, renewal, and net-new motions are separated when they need different plays.

## Buying Committee And Plays

- Buying committee roles, pains, objections, and proof needs are mapped.
- Plays specify message, channel, owner, timing, asset, CTA, and follow-up.
- Engagement scoring reflects account-level behavior, not isolated form fills.
- Metrics include account engagement, meetings, qualified opportunities, pipeline, velocity, win rate, ACV, expansion, and influence.
- Sales follow-up is ready before campaigns launch.
- Personalization uses account-specific insight, business trigger, relationship context, or relevant proof.
- 1:1 plays justify the effort with expected value or strategic importance.
- 1:few campaigns group accounts by shared pain, segment, trigger, or use case, not convenience.
- 1:many programs keep account targeting discipline even when personalization is lighter.

## Operations

- Sales and marketing agree on SLA, owner, sequence, and next action after engagement.
- Suppression rules prevent waste on bad-fit, active opportunity, customer-only, or recently contacted accounts where appropriate.
- Account notes, engagement, campaign touches, and opportunity movement are visible in CRM or the operating system.
- Post-play review captures what worked by account tier, persona, channel, offer, and sales follow-up quality.
