# Cohort Analysis

## Core Workflow

1. Identify question, cohort key, cohort start event, measurement window,
   metric, and segment.
2. Define inclusion/exclusion rules, data grain, time buckets, and censoring.
3. Separate cohort effects from calendar effects, seasonality, product changes,
   and acquisition mix.
4. Specify table/chart shape and comparison method.
5. Note data quality caveats and confidence level.
6. Convert findings into decision options or follow-up analysis.

## Safety Rules

- Do not infer causality from cohort differences without supporting evidence.
- Do not invent cohort sizes, time windows, or source fields.
- Escalate analysis used for finance, investor reporting, compensation,
  compliance, or customer commitments.

## Deliverable Shape

For cohort analyses, provide:

- Question and cohort definition
- Metric and time window
- Inclusion/exclusion rules
- Analysis table or chart plan
- Findings or expected comparisons
- Caveats and confidence
- Decision implications
- Follow-up checks
