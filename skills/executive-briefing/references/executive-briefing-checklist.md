# Executive Briefing Checklist

## Inputs

- Period and audience
- Current priorities and strategy
- Metrics and operating signals
- Customer, product, revenue, finance, people, and delivery updates
- Risks, decisions, blockers, and actions from prior meetings

## Brief Sections

- What changed
- What matters now
- Metrics or signals
- Decisions needed
- Risks and mitigations
- Owner actions
- Open questions

## Quality Checks

- The brief fits the stated decision horizon.
- Facts and assumptions are separate.
- Every requested decision has an owner or next step.
- Metrics cite source context when available.
- The brief avoids generic advice and produces an actionable artifact.
