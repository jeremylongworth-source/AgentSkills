# Character Readability Checklist

Use this checklist for playable characters, enemies, NPCs, creatures, and costumes.

## Readability

- The character reads as a flat silhouette at thumbnail size.
- The design has a clear primary shape family and intentional contrast accents.
- Negative space separates important limbs, props, weapons, or costume shapes.
- The focal area is obvious through contrast, detail density, color, pose, or lighting.
- Interior detail does not compensate for a weak outer shape.

## Role And Gameplay

- The player can infer role, threat, allegiance, class, or function quickly.
- Props, weapons, posture, and body language reinforce gameplay behavior.
- Weak points, interactable parts, hazards, or ability cues are visually clear.
- The character remains readable in motion, in groups, and at gameplay distance.

## Costume And Motifs

- The design has one primary motif, a few supporting motifs, and limited accent colors.
- Material separation is clear: cloth, metal, leather, skin, armor, tech, magic, or organic surfaces.
- Props and costume pieces support silhouette or story instead of adding clutter.
- Variants preserve identity while changing rank, biome, faction, rarity, or progression state.

## Production

- Anatomy and proportions support required animations.
- Hair, cloth, armor, tails, wings, capes, and accessories have a clear rig/simulation plan.
- Material count, texture budget, deformation complexity, and facial needs fit the project.
- The design can be translated into a model sheet, turnaround, or 3D blockout without hidden assumptions.

## Narrative And Tone

- Visual choices match the world's logic and the character's lived role.
- Personality is visible through stance, gear, wear, asymmetry, maintenance, and gesture.
- Cultural, gender, body, and faction cues avoid lazy stereotypes and support the intended tone.
