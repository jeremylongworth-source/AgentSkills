# Game Content Design Checklist

Use this checklist for levels, quests, encounters, progression, and rewards.

## Purpose

- The content has a clear job in the larger player journey.
- The player understands motivation, objective, and stakes.
- The design creates decisions, tradeoffs, mastery, or discovery rather than only instructions.
- The content fits the available mechanics, assets, and production scope.

## Flow

- The opening teaches or frames what matters.
- Escalation introduces, combines, twists, pressures, and resolves mechanics or story ideas.
- Navigation, objective changes, and retry paths are readable.
- Downtime and intensity are intentionally paced.

## Balance

- Difficulty matches the player's expected tools and knowledge.
- Failure states are fair, legible, and recoverable.
- Rewards match effort, risk, rarity, and progression impact.
- Optional content has a reason to exist beyond filler.

## Production

- Required assets, scripting, AI, dialogue, VFX, SFX, and UI support are listed.
- Tuning values are stated as concrete values or ranges.
- Edge cases and sequence breaks are considered.
- Playtest questions identify what must be validated.

## Live Health

- Repetition, grind, exploit potential, economy inflation, and dominant strategies are checked.
- Telemetry or observation points are defined for risky content.
