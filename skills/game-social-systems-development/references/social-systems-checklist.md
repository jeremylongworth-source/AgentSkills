# Social Systems Checklist

Use this checklist for chat, voice, friends, parties, guilds, lobbies, presence, reporting, and moderation.

## Social Design

- The feature has a clear purpose: coordination, discovery, identity, retention, creation, trade, mentorship, or community.
- The intended relationship type is clear: friends, party members, team, guild, matchmade strangers, spectators, followers, or public audience.
- Join, leave, invite, decline, transfer leadership, reconnect, and failure flows are defined.
- Cross-platform identity and platform-specific restrictions are handled.

## Player Controls

- Players can mute, block, report, leave, restrict invites, and manage privacy without hunting through menus.
- Blocked users cannot bypass boundaries through parties, guilds, alts, invites, spectator tools, or shared UGC.
- Communication settings are clear for text, voice, pings, emotes, DMs, friend requests, and group invites.
- Accessibility alternatives exist for voice-only or text-only coordination where needed.

## Safety

- Community standards or feature policies are defined for text, voice, names, profiles, avatars, images, UGC, and behavior.
- Proactive moderation is considered for high-risk content before other players see it.
- Reactive reporting captures enough context for review.
- Human review exists for ambiguous, severe, appealed, or account-impacting decisions.
- Minors, parental controls, consent, data retention, and regional legal requirements are considered.

## Abuse Resistance

- Spam, harassment, impersonation, doxing, grooming, hate speech, cheating coordination, fraud, and exploit sharing are considered.
- Rate limits, cooldowns, trust thresholds, invite limits, and reputation signals are used where appropriate.
- Moderation evasion and repeated offender behavior are tracked.
- Enforcement actions are proportionate and auditable.

## Backend And Operations

- Durable social state lives on trusted services.
- Logs and telemetry support investigation without over-collecting sensitive data.
- Moderation queues have severity, priority, status, actor, evidence, decision, and appeal tracking.
- Customer support and community teams can understand the feature's states and failure modes.
