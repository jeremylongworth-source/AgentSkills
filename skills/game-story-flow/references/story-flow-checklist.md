# Game Story Flow Checklist

Use this checklist for interactive narrative outlines, branching quests, dialogue, and story revisions.

## Agency

- The player's role in the story is clear.
- Major beats give the player a decision, discovery, relationship shift, risk, or new motivation.
- Constraints on player freedom are justified by stakes, context, or gameplay need.
- Consequences are perceivable, even when branches later merge.

## Structure

- The premise, conflict, escalation, midpoint shift, crisis, and resolution are identifiable.
- Each scene changes knowledge, emotion, relationships, resources, location, or world state.
- Story beats support the core gameplay loop rather than interrupting it without purpose.
- Exposition is distributed through play, environment, dialogue, and consequence.

## Branching

- Branches have an explicit design reason.
- Variables, flags, relationship state, and world-state changes are tracked.
- Merge points preserve the meaning of prior choices.
- Branch cost is proportional to production budget and replay value.

## Characters

- Important characters have goals, pressure, contradictions, and change over time.
- Dialogue choices differ by intent or consequence, not only tone.
- Character voice remains consistent under branching conditions.
- Villains, allies, and factions act from understandable incentives.

## Delivery

- Cutscenes, barks, logs, environmental details, and dialogue are assigned to the right delivery channel.
- Passive story moments do not overstay during high-agency gameplay sections.
- Payoffs match earlier promises to the player.
