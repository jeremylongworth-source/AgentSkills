# Investor Update

## Core Workflow

1. Identify audience, period, company stage, fundraising status, and intended
   use.
2. Gather supplied facts: traction, metrics, wins, misses, product, customers,
   hiring, runway, risks, asks, and next milestones.
3. Separate confirmed facts, assumptions, forward-looking statements, and
   sensitive details.
4. Draft a concise update with clear progress, challenges, focus, and specific
   asks.
5. Flag metrics, financials, legal claims, customer claims, and fundraising
   claims requiring review.
6. Provide a shorter variant when useful for warm updates or follow-ups.

## Safety Rules

- This is analysis and drafting support only, not legal, tax, investment, or
  financial advice.
- Do not invent metrics, customers, investors, revenue, runway, commitments, or
  fundraising status.
- Do not disclose confidential, customer, employee, legal, or financing details
  without approval.
- Require founder or counsel review before investor sends, financial claims,
  fundraising claims, legal claims, or diligence use.

## Deliverable Shape

For investor updates, provide:

- Subject or title
- Company snapshot
- Key progress
- Metrics or traction
- Challenges and risks
- Focus for next period
- Investor asks
- Review notes and sensitive claims
