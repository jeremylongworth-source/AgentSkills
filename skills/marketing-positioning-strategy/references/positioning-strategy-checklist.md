# Positioning Strategy Checklist

## Market And Customer

- ICP includes firmographic/demographic fit, pain, urgency, buying power, and reachable channels.
- Personas identify jobs, triggers, objections, decision criteria, and preferred proof.
- Positioning names the alternative the buyer would otherwise choose.
- Exclusions are clear: who the product is not for and why.
- Buying context includes budget source, buying committee, risk, timing, and switching cost.

## Message Quality

- Value proposition connects feature, outcome, metric, and proof.
- Message hierarchy works for website, sales deck, ads, email, and landing pages.
- Claims are specific, defensible, and supported.
- Offer has a clear audience, promise, mechanism, proof, CTA, and risk reducer.
- Messaging uses customer language where possible.
- Differentiation is tied to a meaningful buyer priority, not a cosmetic feature.
- Proof matches claim strength: stronger claims require stronger evidence.

## Validation

- Sales objections and loss reasons are reflected in messaging.
- Customer interviews, reviews, calls, search terms, or CRM notes support the chosen language.
- Landing page and sales material can express the positioning without adding new assumptions.
- Positioning creates focus by making some tactics, audiences, or claims obviously lower priority.
