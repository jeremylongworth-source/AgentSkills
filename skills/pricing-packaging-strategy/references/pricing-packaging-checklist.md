# Pricing Packaging Checklist

## Strategy

- Target segment and buyer are explicit.
- Value metric maps to customer value and business cost/risk.
- Packaging reflects buyer needs, not internal org structure.
- Upgrade paths are clear.
- Free, trial, discount, and enterprise motions have explicit strategy.

## Evidence

- Customer research, win/loss, sales calls, support tickets, competitor pricing, usage data, or conversion data support the recommendation.
- Willingness-to-pay evidence is distinguished from preference or complaint.
- Price sensitivity and value perception are evaluated by segment.
- Competitor pricing is treated as context, not the only anchor.

## Operations

- Billing system can represent the model.
- Taxes, refunds, proration, coupons, invoices, renewals, cancellations, and account changes are considered.
- Pricing page, checkout, CRM, analytics, and sales collateral use consistent definitions.
- Metrics include conversion, ARPA/ARPU, expansion, churn, support burden, gross margin, payback, and customer complaints.

## Validation

- Pricing experiment has a hypothesis, target segment, primary metric, guardrails, and decision rule.
- Existing customers and grandfathering are handled deliberately.
