# QA Test Strategy Checklist

## Scope

- Critical user journeys are named.
- High-risk technical areas are identified.
- Supported platforms, browsers, devices, and integrations are listed.
- Non-goals and excluded coverage are explicit.

## Coverage

- Unit, integration, end-to-end, visual, accessibility, performance, security, compatibility, smoke, regression, and exploratory coverage are considered.
- Automation is used where repeatability and signal justify maintenance cost.
- Manual testing covers judgment-heavy, visual, exploratory, and low-frequency risks.
- Test data and environment setup are realistic enough to catch production issues.

## Release Decision

- Acceptance criteria and release gates are defined.
- Known issues are triaged by user impact and release risk.
- Rollback, monitoring, support, and incident hooks are considered for high-risk releases.
