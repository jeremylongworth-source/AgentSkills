# Reply Drafting Checklist

## Reply Goals

- Acknowledge the customer issue
- Confirm what is understood
- Ask for missing information
- Provide troubleshooting steps
- Explain a policy or limitation
- Share a resolution
- Set expectations for escalation or follow-up

## Quality Checks

- The reply addresses the customer's actual question.
- The first sentence makes clear that the issue was understood.
- Steps are ordered and easy to follow.
- Policy language is accurate and not overconfident.
- The reply avoids internal jargon unless the customer used it first.
- The customer-facing draft contains no internal notes.
- Any uncertainty is clear without sounding evasive.

## Approval Checks

Require human approval before sending when the reply mentions:

- Refunds, credits, discounts, renewals, or invoices
- Account access, permissions, deletion, or data handling
- Security, privacy, compliance, or legal interpretation
- Incident status, uptime, root cause, or remediation timeline
- Product roadmap, feature commitments, or custom work
