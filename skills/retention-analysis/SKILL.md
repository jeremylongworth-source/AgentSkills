# Retention Analysis

## Core Workflow

1. Identify retained entity, retention event, start event, time window, cadence,
   and business question.
2. Define retention type: classic, rolling, return, renewal, revenue, logo, or
   engagement retention.
3. Specify inclusion/exclusion, churn, reactivation, censoring, and segment
   rules.
4. Check data quality, instrumentation, billing/source alignment, and sample
   size.
5. Interpret retention movement with caveats and confidence.
6. Recommend follow-up cohorts, experiments, customer research, or product
   changes.

## Safety Rules

- Do not invent churn, retention, revenue, or renewal figures.
- Do not treat retention changes as causal without evidence.
- Escalate retention outputs used for investor, finance, compensation, or
  public reporting.

## Deliverable Shape

For retention analyses, provide:

- Retention definition
- Entity, start event, and retained event
- Window and cadence
- Segment rules
- Analysis plan or findings
- Caveats and confidence
- Decision implications
- Follow-up checks
