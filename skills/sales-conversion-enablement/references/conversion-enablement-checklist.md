# Sales Conversion Enablement Checklist

## Discovery

- Discovery questions uncover pain, impact, urgency, stakeholders, buying process, constraints, and alternatives.
- Discovery distinguishes stated pain from business impact.
- Current process, trigger event, cost of inaction, and success metric are captured.
- Buyer authority, influence, and internal decision process are not assumed from title alone.

## Conversion Assets

- Demo agenda maps to buyer priorities and success criteria.
- Proposal states problem, solution, value, scope, timeline, proof, price, assumptions, and next steps.
- Objections are categorized and answered with evidence or a clarifying question.
- Mutual action plan has buyer-confirmed dates and owners.
- Legal, security, procurement, technical, and implementation risks are addressed early.
- Handoff notes preserve promised outcomes and risk context.
- Business case quantifies value where possible and states assumptions plainly.
- Competitive comparisons focus on buyer tradeoffs, not feature dumping.
- Follow-up messages restate the buyer's words, decision criteria, and agreed next action.

## Deal Control

- Next step is calendar-specific, buyer-owned where possible, and tied to a milestone.
- Champion, economic buyer, blocker, and evaluator roles are identified for complex deals.
- Close plan includes procurement, legal, security, implementation, and executive approval if relevant.
- Forecast confidence is based on buyer evidence, not seller activity.
