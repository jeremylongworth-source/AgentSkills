# Ticket Triage Checklist

## Inputs to Look For

- Customer message and requested outcome
- Account, plan, region, product area, and environment
- Timeline, frequency, reproduction details, and screenshots or logs
- Business impact, user count, blocked workflow, or deadline
- Prior support history or related incidents

## Classification

- How-to or onboarding
- Bug or regression
- Billing, refund, invoice, or subscription
- Account access, permissions, or identity
- Data import, export, quality, deletion, or privacy
- Incident, outage, degraded performance, or security concern
- Feature request, feedback, cancellation, renewal, or expansion

## Severity Heuristics

- Sev 1: broad outage, data loss, security event, or business-critical block
- Sev 2: major customer workflow blocked with no reasonable workaround
- Sev 3: degraded workflow, partial impact, or workaround available
- Sev 4: question, low-risk request, feedback, or cosmetic issue

Use the customer's actual escalation rules over these heuristics when supplied.

## Routing Checks

- Can support answer directly?
- Is product documentation enough?
- Does engineering need reproduction evidence?
- Does billing, security, legal, or success need ownership?
- Is a customer-facing reply safe with available policy context?
- Is the resolution likely to become a reusable KB article?
